# JOURNAL.md — Like It's 1999

## Day 1 — Finding out about this

Saw the "Like It's 1999" program on Hack Club's site. You boot a real Windows 98 machine in your browser and build a website using only software from before 2000. If you ship it, they mail you a floppy disk with your code. That's hilarious and I want one.

Deadline is July 13 at 5PM Eastern. Tight but doable.

**The plan:** A personal homepage / AoT fansite. Full 90s aesthetic — marquees, guestbook, visitor counter, flaming text GIFs, the works.

**Tools I'll use inside Win98:**
- EditPlus for HTML
- Paint Shop Pro for graphics
- Netscape to test

---

## Day 2 — Building it

Booted the emulator at 99.hackclub.com. It actually works — real Windows 98 with the classic grey taskbar and everything.

**What went right:**
- EditPlus is straightforward. Color-coded HTML, line numbers, all you really need
- Paint Shop Pro is janky but works. Made a banner and some buttons
- The emulator saves state so I can come back to it

**What went wrong:**
- The emulator is slow. Takes a good 10 seconds to open anything
- Paint Shop Pro crashes if I try to do too much at once — learned to save early and often
- No copy-paste between the emulator and my real computer. Have to type everything inside
- Fonts are ugly. But that's period-accurate so it works

**The site so far:**
- index.html with a marquee title, animated GIF banner, and links to other pages
- guestbook.html with a form
- Hit counter graphic that I made in Paint Shop Pro (it doesn't actually count anything but it looks legit)

---

## What I Learned

- People in 1999 were patient. Waiting 10 seconds for a program to open was normal
- Making a website with just a text editor and Paint Shop Pro forces you to keep it simple
- No flexbox. No CSS frameworks. Just tables, font tags, and center tags. It actually works
- The floppy disk reward is worth the effort — physical media is cool
